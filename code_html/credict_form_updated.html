6.4 SWOT Analysis & Mitigations

Strength: The company has a diversified business profile with operations across four continents and businesses in approximately 90 countries. It maintains financial stability through a strong balance sheet, ensuring access to internal and external funds for growth opportunities. Operational efficiency is achieved by focusing on both variable and fixed costs across all entities. The company has a significant market share and position, supported by strategic customer relationships and contractual tie-ups.

Opportunity: There is an opportunity to enhance cash position due to ongoing improvement in demand scenarios. Strategic partnerships and strengthening R&D can capture market opportunities, particularly in export markets like the United States and Kenya.

Weakness: The company faces competition and needs to continuously monitor import trends to adjust sales strategies and remain competitive. Process efficiency can be improved to further optimize operations.

Threats: Non-Performing Assets (NPA) and market and economic conditions pose threats to the company. Global geopolitical unrest, cyclicality of demand, trade tariff conflicts, and energy price fluctuations are significant risks. Geopolitical issues can impact strategic growth trajectory and stakeholder value.

Mitigation: The company has formulated strategic plans and objectives for risk management, including a detailed risk management policy covering risk identification, mitigation measures, and business continuity plans. Efforts are made to scale up export markets and strengthen distribution networks. The company continuously reviews its risk appetite and strategy relating to key risks, including market, cyber security, product, and reputational risks, with guidelines, policies, and processes for monitoring and mitigating such risks.
